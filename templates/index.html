Explore SDSS Galaxy Classification with Machine Learning

Welcome to the galaxy classification tool. Use our machine learning model to classify galaxies based on their photometric data. Dive into the universe of data and predictions.

The Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) has explored about one-third of the sky and discovered around 1 billion objects, including almost 3 million galaxies. This dataset contains 100,000 rows of photometric image data, with each galaxy classified into one of two subclasses: 'STARFORMING' or 'STARBURST'. This classification helps in understanding the various stages of galaxy evolution and their physical properties.

How It Works

Discover how our galaxy classification tool works to provide accurate predictions on galaxy types. Follow these simple steps:

1. Input Data

Enter the photometric data of your galaxy into the form below. This data is essential for our model to make a precise classification. Ensure you provide all required details for the best results.

2. Submit Your Data

Click the 'Submit' button to send your data to our model. The submission process securely transmits your information for analysis.

3. Data Processing

Our advanced machine learning model processes the submitted data. Trained on the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) dataset, the model analyzes the characteristics of your galaxy to determine its subclass.

4. Receive Prediction

Once processing is complete, you will receive a prediction indicating whether the galaxy is classified as 'STARFORMING' or 'STARBURST'. Review the results to gain insights into the galaxy’s type.
